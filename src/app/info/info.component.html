The Impact of Climate Change on Drought in Catalonia Catalonia, a region of Spain, has been intensely affected by drought in recent years, a phenomenon directly related to climate change and global warming. As temperatures have risen, rainfall patterns have become erratic, leading to a significant decrease in precipitation. This has resulted in a substantial reduction of water resources, affecting reservoirs, rivers, and aquifers. Since 2020, the region has faced extreme droughts, with levels of stored water dropping to historical lows. This phenomenon impacts agriculture, which relies on irrigation, and also jeopardizes the water supply for cities while affecting local biodiversity. Global warming has altered the water cycle, causing more prolonged and severe droughts, which exacerbates environmental challenges in Catalonia and highlights the urgency of taking action to mitigate the effects of climate change.
